That Time I Saved Harry Potter

So Harry basically went dark on us all summer, which, red flag. Turns out he was locked in his room by his absolutely dreadful aunt and uncle, and a weird little house-elf named Dobby had been sabotaging his mail because he thought Harry going back to Hogwarts would get him killed. Very sweet of Dobby honestly, very unhinged plan. Naturally, me and Ron looked at each other and were like "cool, we're stealing Mr. Weasley's car." No permission asked. No permission granted. We just took it.

Flying a car for the first time at midnight with zero lessons is exactly as chill as it sounds, which is to say not chill at all. We show up at Privet Drive, Harry's staring at us through a barred window like we're his personal miracle, and we just start yanking the bars off with a rope tied to the bumper like we do this every day. Uncle Vernon is downstairs absolutely losing it, we're shoving Harry's trunk and his owl Hedwig through the window, and the second we peel away into the sky all three of us just lost it laughing. Ten out of ten rescue, would recommend, mildly illegal.

Then, a few days later, King's Cross just says no. Me and Ron full-on faceplant into the barrier to Platform Nine and Three-Quarters in front of a bunch of very confused Muggles, and the platform's sealed shut, and the train is literally leaving without us. So obviously the only logical next step is to fly the car to Scotland. We're dodging clouds, we're dodging a genuinely offended flock of geese, Harry's in the back yelling "DO YOU KNOW HOW TO LAND THIS THING" and the honest answer was no. We did not know how to land the thing. We found that out real quick when we nosedived straight into the Whomping Willow, which took it about as well as a tree that hits people usually does. We crawled out banged up, missing a headlight, and cracking up, and I remember thinking there was no way the rest of the year could top that entrance.
